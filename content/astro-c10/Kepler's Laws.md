# Kepler’s Laws

## Kepler’s First Law

Planetary orbits are ellipses with the Sun at one focus.

## Kepler’s Second Law

A line between the Sun and a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times.

When the planet is closer to the Sun, it must therefore move more quickly, since the area of the ellipse closer to the Sun is smaller.

## Kepler’s Third Law

The square of a planet’s orbital period is proportional to the cube of its semimajor axis:

$$
P^2 = kR^3
$$
