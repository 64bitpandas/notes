## Effects on Visual Perception and Thought

- Müller-Lyer illusion: only affects Western cultures ⟷ ⟩—⟨
    - homogeny of illusion: carpenters

- Spatial reference:
    - egocentric orientation (left/right relative to self) → box + stick experiment
    - vs allocentric orientation: NSEW

- holistic thought: self is dependant on group
    - object position/orientation evaluated in context of one another

- analytic thought: detachment of self from group
    - independence
    - Western way of thinking

### dichotomy of thought
- 85% holistic/allocentric, people interconnected
- 15% analytic/individualistic

- economic behavior
    - ultimatum game: proposer gets x money to accepter, if accepter rejects then nobody gets any money
        - rational: always accept (receiver), offer as little as possible (proposer)
        - WEIRD: 50% offered on avg, <20% rejected half the time
        - mean offer dependent on culture, market integration
            - more market trading → higher offer
    - dictator game: same as ultimatum, but must accept
        - rational: give zero
    - public goods game: put in x% to public, gets doubled, distributed equally
        - rational: give 100%, everyone gets double

## Non-Western Cultures

### Aché
- Amazon forest foragers/hunters
- risky hunting, meat shared equally among households
- sharing = normal, boasting = taboo
- Ultimatum: 40-50% mean, no offers rejected

### Orma
- pastoralists in Kenya (livestock)
- harambee tradition: local fund for public good (similar to taxes)
- Ultimatum: very generous, 58% mean

### Au/Gnau
- culturally diverse societies in Papua New Guinea
- Ultimatum: >50% offers, but many rejected
- Melanesian gift giving culture: gifts are an obligation to reciprocate
    - Norm: many small gifts, instead of large gifts
- hunting, foraging, horticulture (gardening)

### Hadza
- forager society in Africa / hunting
- share meat, but consider contributions a burden (opposite of Aché)
- Enforced by social sanctions (→ shaming, gossip), will try to get around it if needed
- Ultimatum: larger camps = larger offers, small offers rejected

### Machiguenga
- foragers in South America
- cooperation restricted to small **family units** (no personal names)
    - ↳ economically independent
- no stranger interaction
- Ultimatum: lowest offers (22%)

### Lamalera
- whale hunting, foraging
- culture of dividing spoils from large prey
- hunting is risky, requires large crew + cooperation
- specific names for dividing cuts of whale
- Ultimatum: treated like whale, divided equally (58% mean - very high)

#### 4 level analysis:
- ontogeny: influenced by societal norms
- mechanism: high activation of anterior insula, dorsolateral prefrontal cortex
- function: culture evolved to support hunting large prey
- phylogeny: humans are generally prosocial
